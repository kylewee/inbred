üîß Labor Rates: The Foundation of Auto Repair Pricing

Why labor rates vary so much:

  • Location matters: Labor rates in the U.S. range from $127/hour in Vermont to $152/hour in Wyoming, based on local cost of living
  • Shop type affects pricing: Dealerships typically charge 20-40% more than independent shops
  • Overhead costs: Rent, insurance, equipment, licensing, and employee benefits are all built into the hourly rate

üî© Parts Pricing: What You Need to Know

How shops handle parts markup:

  • Standard markup: Most shops mark up parts 30-100% above their cost
  • Inventory risk: Shops need to cover the cost of stocking parts that might sit on shelves
  • Shipping & handling: Rush delivery, core charges, and taxes add to part costs
  • Warranty coverage: Markup helps cover warranty replacements and returns
